The Future of Education has arrived

27 September Christina Montgomery Curriculum, Education 0 Comments

What is personalised learning?

One of the problems with mainstream education is that it relies on students learning at the same pace, in the same way. Most schools do not accommodate the fact that some students need extra attention whereas others flourish when given more leeway. They also don’t allow for different teaching and learning methods, which means that some students "slip through the cracks" when they don’t respond to a conventional approach.

The best combination is great teachers working with technology to engage students in the pursuit of the learning they actually need.

Arne Duncan

Essentially, personalised learning provides students with a constant flow of feedback in order to assist them in guiding and managing their own learning experience. Teachers have more of a supervisory role than a lecturing one. In this way, students become more self-reliant, learning how to manage their own time and set their own goals. Students tend to becomes more confident in themselves and their abilities, providing them with essential life skills and a mindset prepared for the world beyond school. In some schools implementing the personalised learning approach, teachers will be assigned students to oversee and mentor for the entire duration of their high school education. The element of permanence allows for a more personal relationship to develop between learners and educators, providing teachers with a holistic understanding of students’ growth and progress over time. Change to traditional methods of teaching on a widespread scale takes time. For now, personalised learning is limited to schools that can afford the cutting edge technology required to enable students to work flexibly. Private education, therefore, will be the first to see this approach proliferate.
